# Capability: problem-needs

## What it is

The product-side problem framing for FastPPT: teachers need faster lesson preparation, but not at the cost of correctness, control, or source reuse.

## Read when

- deciding roadmap priority
- checking competition alignment
- arguing for or against a feature

## Source of truth

- `docs/product/problem-needs.md`

## Current status

- `active`
- Established enough to guide prioritization
- Still benefits from more teacher samples

## Key conclusions

- The highest-value scenario is updating or extending existing teaching material, not generating arbitrary decks from zero.
- Teachers care more about controllability and low rework than pure automation.
- Generation must fit actual classroom constraints: duration, audience, difficult points, and teaching flow.

## Upstream / downstream

- Upstream: teacher interviews and teacher feedback
- Downstream: product positioning, Mode A priority, revise loop design
